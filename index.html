This dish is a great example of throw everything in the pan and add some garlic, which really is the best way to prepare any meal. It got its name from where I bought a lot of these ingredients: the Woodstock Farmer’s Market in Woodstock, Illinois, which supplies its visitors with fresh, home-grown fruits, vegetables, pastries, and everything in-between from the farmers and producers themselves. You do not, of course, need to visit Woodstock to make this dish, but I always encourage the support of your local farm or market – if you are able to buy your ingredients at one of those, please do. Recipe written and coded by Molly McCafferty in September 2021.

The Rice

I like to prepare my rice beforehand since I am a poor multitasker when it comes to cooking, but feel free to save time and do everything at once.

  1. In a 5 quart pot, bring 4 cups of water to a steady boil and pour in 4 cups of rice.

  2. Stir until the rice has been thoroughly drenched. Follow any additional instructions on your package or box of rice.

  3. When it has finished cooking, throw black pepper and 2-3 pads of butter into that pot and stir. Let sit while you prepare the other ingredients.

The Good Stuff

  1. In a medium to large pan and over medium-low heat, add 1 pad of butter and garlic until you, and potentially your neighbors, can smell the garlic cooking.

  2. On medium heat, add the jalapeño peppers and mushrooms.

  3. Let cook, stirring occasionally. When the mushrooms begin to brown and the peppers are aromatic, add the chicken.

  4. Make sure you are using cooked chicken, and if previously frozen, make sure it has been defrosted. It can still be cold, but should not be iced over.

  5. Let the chicken cook for 1.5 minutes or until relatively tender.

  6. Over medium-low heat, add soy sauce and stir until everything in the pot has been coated. Adjust heat as necessary depending on how cooked the chicken pieces are.

  7. Add spinach last, and let it cook down until small and dark.

  8. Stir together until everything appears to be well-cooked and tender.

  9. Add your jalapeño salt. If you kept the seeds with your peppers, go easy on the salt. If not, go nuts until you reach your spice tolerance level.

  10. Let sit for 3 to 5 minutes, and pour into a serving dish or keep in the pan. Whichever is more your style.

  11. Have handy your pot of rice, give everything a healthy stir, and serve.

  12. 3 large spoonfuls of rice to 2 good spoonfuls of chicken is a good proportion.

  13. Serves 3 people, with leftover rice. Enjoy!
